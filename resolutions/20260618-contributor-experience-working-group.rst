==================================================
 2026-06-18 Contributor Experience Working Group
==================================================

OpenStack's contributor base has narrowed over the past decade,
even as the project's deployment footprint has grown.
The community's mechanisms for recognizing non-code contributions,
onboarding new contributors, and tracking community health have not
kept pace. The `Active Contributor exception process`_ sees negligible
use outside of the i18n SIG, despite its intent to recognize bug
triagers, documentation writers, event organizers, and others whose
work does not produce commits in repositories. The `First Contact SIG`_
has not had sustained activity in several years. The Upstream Institute
has not held training sessions since 2020. No single entity within
OpenStack's governance is responsible for the end-to-end contributor
experience.

The OpenStack Technical Committee establishes a Contributor Experience
Working Group (CEWG) as a TC working group with a mandate to improve
the contributor experience across the OpenStack community. The working
group is delegated authority over three areas:

Contributor Recognition
  Making non-code contributions visible and ensuring that the people
  that contribute to the OpenStack community in ways including and
  beyond writing code are recognized within the project's governance.

Community Health
  Understanding the state of OpenStack's contributor base: who
  contributes, in what roles, how retention and responsiveness
  are trending, and where the community's capacity is concentrated
  or at risk.

Contributor Onboarding
  Owning the strategy for how new contributors find their way into
  the project, absorbing the mission of the First Contact SIG. The
  working group will propose the formal archival of the First Contact
  SIG as a separate governance change. If the Upstream Institute is
  revived, the CEWG will serve as its governance home.

The working group does not have authority to modify the TC charter.
Any changes to AC definitions or election rules require the normal
charter amendment process. The working group does not own code
repositories, CI infrastructure, or release processes; those belong
to project teams and the TaCT SIG. The CEWG will coordinate with
the `Election working group`_ to propose any changes that affect voter
eligibility.

Membership and Operations
=========================

The working group shall have a minimum of three members, at least one
of whom must be a current TC member. Members serve for one release cycle
and may volunteer to continue. The working group will operate on the
``openstack-discuss`` mailing list using the ``[contributor-experience]``
subject tag and will provide status updates at TC meetings frequently.

If the working group cannot maintain its minimum membership for two
consecutive cycles, the TC will dissolve it or redistribute its responsibilities.
